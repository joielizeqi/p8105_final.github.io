our project!

Although dogs are often referred to as "people’s best friend," dog bite injuries remain a significant public health concern. From 2005 to 2013, an average of 337,103 emergency department (ED) visits per year were attributed to dog bites in the United States. In 2018 alone, nearly 27,000 individuals required reconstructive surgery as a result of dog bite-related injuries.

Welcome to
our project!

Our project aims to investigate the complex relationships between dog bite incidents and various factors, such as dog characteristics, geographical distribution, and trends over time. By examining dog bite rates in different zip code areas, we hope to pinpoint potential hotspots that can inform public safety measures. We’ll also look into whether specific traits of dogs—like breed, age, gender, and neuter status—are linked to how often bites occur. This analysis could help in developing targeted strategies to reduce the risk of dog bites.

The demographics of dog bites in the United States

Dog bites are a significant public health issue. There is no comprehensive study of dog bite demographics. It was the purpose of this study to perform such an analysis across the US. The National Electronic Injury Surveillance System All Injury Program data for the years 2005 through 2013 was accessed; dog bite injuries were extracted and analyzed. Statistical analyses were performed with SUDAAN 11.0.01™ software to account for the weighted, stratified nature of the data.
